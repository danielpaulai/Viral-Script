# 50 VIRAL HOOKS DATABASE

## How This Works

Each hook has:
- **TYPE** - The hook category
- **TEMPLATE** - The fill-in-the-blank formula
- **EXAMPLE** - A ready-to-use script opener
- **WHY IT WORKS** - The psychology behind it

---

## HOOK TYPE 1: PERSONAL EXPERIENCE (Hooks 1-8)

*Present your unique perspective to build instant credibility and relatability.*

---

### HOOK #1: The Painful Past
**Template:** "I used to [painful thing everyone relates to]."
**Example:** "I used to write a script, film, edit, and post all in one day."
**Why it works:** Viewers think "that's me right now" and stay to see the solution.

---

### HOOK #2: The Realization Moment
**Template:** "I spent [time period] doing [thing] before I realized [insight]."
**Example:** "I spent 3 years posting daily before I realized I was burning out for nothing."
**Why it works:** Implies hard-earned wisdom they can get in 60 seconds.

---

### HOOK #3: The Unlikely Start
**Template:** "I [started from unlikely place] and now [impressive result]."
**Example:** "I came out here lonely. Didn't know anybody. Had to build from scratch."
**Why it works:** Creates a transformation arc viewers want to follow.

---

### HOOK #4: The Confession
**Template:** "I'm going to be honest - I [vulnerable admission]."
**Example:** "I'm going to be honest - I almost quit content creation last year."
**Why it works:** Vulnerability creates instant trust and curiosity.

---

### HOOK #5: The Accidental Discovery
**Template:** "I accidentally discovered [thing] when I [situation]."
**Example:** "I accidentally discovered the best hook formula when I analyzed my flops."
**Why it works:** Makes the insight feel serendipitous and authentic.

---

### HOOK #6: The Rule Breaker
**Template:** "I broke every rule about [topic] and here's what happened."
**Example:** "I broke every rule about posting times and here's what happened."
**Why it works:** Rebellion is intriguing. People want to see if rules are real.

---

### HOOK #7: The Big Result
**Template:** "I just [achieved impressive thing]."
**Example:** "I just sold my company for $10 million."
**Why it works:** Immediate credibility. Viewers want to know how.

---

### HOOK #8: The Time Marker
**Template:** "[Time period] ago I [past state]. Today I [current state]."
**Example:** "6 months ago I had 200 followers. Today I have 200,000."
**Why it works:** Concrete transformation with a timeline feels achievable.

---

## HOOK TYPE 2: CASE STUDY (Hooks 9-16)

*Highlight someone/something achieving unexpected results.*

---

### HOOK #9: The Brand Spotlight
**Template:** "[Brand/Person] is doing something nobody's talking about."
**Example:** "Adobe is doing something with AI that nobody's talking about."
**Why it works:** Name recognition + exclusivity = instant attention.

---

### HOOK #10: The Unexpected Winner
**Template:** "This [underdog] is outperforming [expected winner] and here's why."
**Example:** "This unknown creator is outperforming Mr. Beast's engagement and here's why."
**Why it works:** David vs Goliath stories are irresistible.

---

### HOOK #11: The Number Flex
**Template:** "[Person/Brand] hit [impressive number] by doing [unexpected thing]."
**Example:** "This marketer hits 40% conversion rates by ditching landing pages."
**Why it works:** Specific numbers create believability and curiosity.

---

### HOOK #12: The Reverse Engineering
**Template:** "I studied [successful person] for [time] and found their secret."
**Example:** "I studied Alex Hormozi for 6 months and found his secret."
**Why it works:** Positions you as a researcher delivering exclusive insights.

---

### HOOK #13: The Industry Insider
**Template:** "[X]% of [group] are doing [thing] and you're not."
**Example:** "74% of artists are experimenting with AI and you're not."
**Why it works:** FOMO + social proof is a powerful combo.

---

### HOOK #14: The Method Reveal
**Template:** "Here's exactly how [person] [achieved result] in [timeframe]."
**Example:** "Here's exactly how Brandon Baum creates impossible videos in minutes."
**Why it works:** "Exactly how" promises actionable, specific steps.

---

### HOOK #15: The Comparison
**Template:** "Most [people] get [bad result]. [This person] gets [great result]."
**Example:** "Most websites convert at 2-4%. This marketer hits 20-40%."
**Why it works:** Gap between normal and exceptional creates desire.

---

### HOOK #16: The Pattern Interrupt
**Template:** "[Famous person/brand] just did something that changes everything."
**Example:** "Forbes just said something about AI that changes everything."
**Why it works:** Authority + urgency = must-watch content.

---

## HOOK TYPE 3: SECRET REVEAL (Hooks 17-26)

*Tease insider knowledge that makes viewers feel like they're getting exclusive access.*

---

### HOOK #17: The Hidden Truth
**Template:** "Here's something nobody tells you about [topic]."
**Example:** "Here's something nobody tells you about self-respect."
**Why it works:** Implies insider knowledge most people miss.

---

### HOOK #18: The Industry Secret
**Template:** "The [industry] doesn't want you to know this."
**Example:** "The algorithm doesn't want you to know this."
**Why it works:** Creates an us-vs-them dynamic with viewer on your side.

---

### HOOK #19: The Future Warning
**Template:** "What's coming in [timeframe] is going to [big change]."
**Example:** "What's coming in 2025 is going to destroy most creators."
**Why it works:** Future-pacing creates urgency and fear of missing out.

---

### HOOK #20: The Rate of Change
**Template:** "The rate at which [thing] is [changing] is actually [emotion]."
**Example:** "The rate at which AI is progressing is actually terrifying."
**Why it works:** Emotional language + trending topic = engagement.

---

### HOOK #21: The Counterintuitive Truth
**Template:** "The real reason [thing happens] has nothing to do with [expected reason]."
**Example:** "The real reason your content flops has nothing to do with the algorithm."
**Why it works:** Challenges assumptions and promises new perspective.

---

### HOOK #22: The Insider Access
**Template:** "I have information about [topic] that most people don't."
**Example:** "I have information about where social media is going that most people don't."
**Why it works:** Exclusivity is magnetic. People want to be "in the know."

---

### HOOK #23: The Uncomfortable Truth
**Template:** "No one wants to hear this but [hard truth]."
**Example:** "No one wants to hear this but your content isn't the problem."
**Why it works:** Frames the message as brave truth-telling.

---

### HOOK #24: The Prediction
**Template:** "In [timeframe], [current thing] will be [new state]."
**Example:** "In 12 months, perfect content will be everywhere and we'll be sick of it."
**Why it works:** Predictions feel valuable and shareable.

---

### HOOK #25: The Strategy Reveal
**Template:** "Here's how [international/unexpected group] are [achieving result]."
**Example:** "Here's how international creators charge US prices."
**Why it works:** Geographic/demographic angle adds novelty.

---

### HOOK #26: The Liberation
**Template:** "Here's the truth that will set you free about [topic]."
**Example:** "Here's the truth that will set you free: no one was watching as much as you thought."
**Why it works:** Promises emotional relief and breakthrough.

---

## HOOK TYPE 4: CONTRARIAN (Hooks 27-34)

*Challenge conventional beliefs to stop the scroll.*

---

### HOOK #27: The Direct Attack
**Template:** "[Common advice] is ruining your [desired outcome]."
**Example:** "Educational content is ruining your sales."
**Why it works:** Attacks something they're probably doing. Must defend or learn.

---

### HOOK #28: The Opposite Claim
**Template:** "Stop [thing everyone says to do]."
**Example:** "Stop posting educational content on Instagram."
**Why it works:** Contradicts mainstream advice. Demands explanation.

---

### HOOK #29: The Myth Buster
**Template:** "Everything you've been told about [topic] is wrong."
**Example:** "Everything you've been told about going viral is wrong."
**Why it works:** Promises paradigm shift in understanding.

---

### HOOK #30: The Unpopular Opinion
**Template:** "Unpopular opinion: [controversial take]."
**Example:** "Unpopular opinion: consistency is overrated."
**Why it works:** Signals bold thinking. Invites debate and shares.

---

### HOOK #31: The Anti-Advice
**Template:** "The worst advice I ever got was [common advice]."
**Example:** "The worst advice I ever got was 'just be consistent.'"
**Why it works:** Story format + contrarian angle = compelling combo.

---

### HOOK #32: The Inversion
**Template:** "[Desired outcome] doesn't come from [expected source]."
**Example:** "Wealth doesn't come from working harder."
**Why it works:** Disrupts cause-effect assumptions they hold.

---

### HOOK #33: The Paradox
**Template:** "The more you [common action], the less you [desired result]."
**Example:** "The more you post, the less you grow."
**Why it works:** Paradoxes are mentally sticky and shareable.

---

### HOOK #34: The Industry Lie
**Template:** "[Industry/gurus] have been lying to you about [topic]."
**Example:** "Marketing gurus have been lying to you about funnels."
**Why it works:** Positions viewer as victim who needs your truth.

---

## HOOK TYPE 5: QUESTION (Hooks 35-42)

*Frame an opening question that demands an answer.*

---

### HOOK #35: The Direct Question
**Template:** "Why do most [people] fail at [thing]?"
**Example:** "Why do most businesses die from too much, not too little?"
**Why it works:** Viewer's brain automatically tries to answer.

---

### HOOK #36: The "What If" Question
**Template:** "What if [common belief] was actually [opposite]?"
**Example:** "What if posting more was actually hurting your growth?"
**Why it works:** Opens imagination to new possibilities.

---

### HOOK #37: The Choice Question
**Template:** "Would you rather [option A] or [option B]?"
**Example:** "Would you rather 10 years of pain for one big exit or mini wins every year?"
**Why it works:** Forces mental engagement and self-reflection.

---

### HOOK #38: The Identification Question
**Template:** "Do you know what type of [category] you are?"
**Example:** "Do you know what type of niche you're in - visual, verbal, or product?"
**Why it works:** Curiosity about self-categorization is powerful.

---

### HOOK #39: The Problem Question
**Template:** "Ever wonder why [frustrating thing happens]?"
**Example:** "Ever wonder why your videos get views but no followers?"
**Why it works:** Names their pain point directly.

---

### HOOK #40: The Rhetorical Challenge
**Template:** "What would happen if you [bold action]?"
**Example:** "What would happen if you just stopped caring what people think?"
**Why it works:** Plants a seed of possibility in their mind.

---

### HOOK #41: The Knowledge Test
**Template:** "Can you name [number] [things] that [outcome]?"
**Example:** "Can you name 3 substances that permanently change your personality?"
**Why it works:** Challenges their knowledge. They stay to verify.

---

### HOOK #42: The Stakes Question
**Template:** "What's the real cost of [common behavior]?"
**Example:** "What's the real cost of staying in your hometown?"
**Why it works:** Forces them to confront consequences they avoid.

---

## HOOK TYPE 6: LIST (Hooks 43-47)

*Introduce a numbered list that promises organized value.*

---

### HOOK #43: The Countdown
**Template:** "[Number] [things] that will [outcome]."
**Example:** "3 substances that can permanently change your personality."
**Why it works:** Specific number sets clear expectation. Easy to consume.

---

### HOOK #44: The Daily Tracker
**Template:** "Day [number] of [challenge/goal]."
**Example:** "Day 29 of building my business in public."
**Why it works:** Serialized content creates return viewers.

---

### HOOK #45: The Toolkit
**Template:** "[Number] tools I use to [achieve result]."
**Example:** "3 AI tools I use to never run out of content ideas."
**Why it works:** Tool lists are highly saveable and shareable.

---

### HOOK #46: The Mistake List
**Template:** "[Number] mistakes killing your [desired outcome]."
**Example:** "5 mistakes killing your engagement right now."
**Why it works:** Fear of loss is stronger than desire for gain.

---

### HOOK #47: The Ranking
**Template:** "The [number] most [adjective] [things] for [outcome]."
**Example:** "The 3 most underrated strategies for going viral."
**Why it works:** Rankings promise curated, valuable information.

---

## HOOK TYPE 7: EDUCATION (Hooks 48-50)

*Introduce a step-by-step process that promises transformation.*

---

### HOOK #48: The How-To
**Template:** "Here's how I [achieve result] in [timeframe]."
**Example:** "Here's how I steal viral video formats legally."
**Why it works:** "How I" adds personal proof to the promise.

---

### HOOK #49: The Step-by-Step
**Template:** "The exact [number]-step process to [outcome]."
**Example:** "The exact 3-step process to find viral content in any niche."
**Why it works:** "Exact" and "step" promise specificity and ease.

---

### HOOK #50: The Template Reveal
**Template:** "I'm giving you the exact template I use to [outcome]."
**Example:** "I'm giving you the exact template I use to write scripts in 5 minutes."
**Why it works:** Templates are the most saveable content type.

# QUICK REFERENCE: HOOK SELECTION GUIDE

| If your content is about... | Use these hooks |
|----------------------------|-----------------|
| Your personal story | #1-8 (Personal Experience) |
| Someone else's success | #9-16 (Case Study) |
| Industry insights/trends | #17-26 (Secret Reveal) |
| Challenging common beliefs | #27-34 (Contrarian) |
| Making people think | #35-42 (Question) |
| Tips, tools, or steps | #43-47 (List) |
| Teaching a process | #48-50 (Education) |

# COPY-PASTE HOOK TEMPLATES

## For Business Content:
- "I just [big result]. Here's the [number] things that got me there."
- "Most [businesses/creators] fail because of [unexpected reason]."
- "[Number]% of [industry] are doing [thing] wrong."

## For Personal Growth Content:
- "Here's something nobody tells you about [self-improvement topic]."
- "I spent [time] doing [thing] before I realized [insight]."
- "The real reason you're [struggling with thing] has nothing to do with [expected cause]."

## For Tech/AI Content:
- "The rate at which [technology] is advancing is [emotion]."
- "[Big company] just did something that changes everything."
- "In [timeframe], [current thing] will be [future state]."

## For Social Media Growth:
- "Here's how I [result] without [expected requirement]."
- "Stop [common advice]. Here's what works instead."
- "I studied [number] viral videos and found [pattern]."
